title: "Yiddish Word Classes: An Overview of Parts of Speech"
description: "Learn how Yiddish words are grouped into word classes (וואָרטקלאַסן), how to identify them, and how some words belong to multiple classes."
---

Every word you speak or write in Yiddish belongs to a **וואָרטקלאַס** (word class). Yiddish divides its vocabulary into distinct classes, each with its own grammatical rules for how words change form and behave in a sentence. Understanding word classes gives you a foundation for every other topic in Yiddish grammar.

## The eight word classes

### זאכווערטער (nouns)

**זאכווערטער** — also called **סובסטאנטיוון** or **אביעקטן**, and in Hebrew **שם־דבר** — are names of things, people, or other living beings. Examples include: באבע, בוך, הויז, מענטש, שפראך, טאטע, מאמע.

You can identify a noun by checking whether it can take an article (דער, די, דאס, or דעם) before it. If it can, it is a noun. Nouns also inflect for number: **טיש – טישן**, **מענטש – מענטשן**, **וועלט – וועלטן**.

### ארטיקלען (articles)

**ארטיקלען** is a small word class consisting of just a few words:

> דער, די, דאס, דעם, אַ, אַן, קיין

Articles appear before nouns to mark them. **דער, די, דאס, דעם** are **באשטימטע ארטיקלען** (definite articles — equivalent to Hebrew's ה׳ הידוע). **אַ, אַן, קיין** are **אומבאשטימטע ארטיקלען** (indefinite articles).

Because articles can only appear before nouns, they are often grouped together with the noun class.

### ווערבן (verbs)

**ווערבן** are words that denote an action — something that is done. Examples: זאגן, קומען, גיין, לערנען, מאכן, שטיין.

Verbs can be **טראנזיטיווע** (transitive — their action can apply to an object: מאכן **א קאווע**, לערנען **א מוסר ספר**) or **אומטראנזיטיווע** (intransitive — their action cannot: שטיין, וואקסן, ווארטן).

You can identify a verb by checking whether it inflects for tense: past (**האט געזאגט**), present (**זאגט**), and future (**וועט זאגן**).

### אדיעקטיוון (adjectives)

**אדיעקטיוון** describe properties of nouns. Examples: א **גוטער** דאקטאר, **דינע** מצות, א **טיפער** רעיון.

The distinguishing mark of an adjective is that it can take the endings **־ע, ־ער, ־ערע,** or **־ערער**. For example: ריין → ריינע, ריינער, ריינערע, ריינערער.

### אדווערבן (adverbs)

**אדווערבן** tell you how, where, when, how intensely, or how often a verb is performed — much as adjectives do for nouns. Nearly all adjectives also function as adverbs (e.g., **גוט** in **גוט פארשטיין**). Words that can only modify verbs and not nouns are called pure adverbs: דא, דארט, באלד, פלוצלינג, ווידער, אינגאנצן.

Names of days, holidays, seasons (שבת, זומער, ווינטער, יום־טוב), and time expressions (אמאל, יעצט, דערנאך) are also adverbs.

### פראנאמען (pronouns)

**פראנאמען** stand in for people and sometimes things when they are not called by name:

> איך, דו, ער, זי, מיר, איר, עטס, זיי, עס, מען

### צאלווערטער (numerals)

**צאלווערטער** are simply numbers: **איינס, צוויי, דריי**, and so on.

### קאניונקציעס (conjunctions) and פרעפאזיציעס (prepositions)

**קאניונקציעס** connect one sentence or word to another: אבער, אדער, אויב, אויך, און, אז, ווייל, כדי, נאר.

**פרעפאזיציעס** introduce indirect objects into a sentence: אין, אויף, מיט, פון, צו, ביי, דורך, וועגן, קעגן.

## אינוואריאנטן — words that never change

**אדווערבן, פרעפאזיציעס,** and **קאניונקציעס** are often grouped together under the name **אינוואריאנטן** (invariants). Unlike nouns, adjectives, and verbs, invariants never inflect — they always remain in the same form.

<Info>
Nouns, adjectives, and verbs all "bend" (בייגן זיך) according to grammatical rules. Invariants never bend, which is why distinguishing between prepositions, conjunctions, and adverbs can be tricky — they all look the same regardless of context.
</Info>

## How to identify which class a word belongs to

Each word class has a reliable test:

| Word class | Test |
|------------|------|
| **זאכווארט** | Can take an article (דער/די/דאס/דעם) before it; can form a plural |
| **ווערב** | Can appear in past, present, and future tense |
| **אדיעקטיוו** | Can take the endings ־ע, ־ער, ־ערע, or ־ערער directly (without adding another suffix first) |
| **אינוואריאנט** | Never changes form under any circumstances |

<Tip>
In Yiddish, distinguishing between prepositions, conjunctions, and adverbs is genuinely difficult — even for native speakers — precisely because all three are invariants. Don't be discouraged if they look the same; the difference lies in their grammatical role within the sentence.
</Tip>

## Words that belong to multiple classes

Many Yiddish words belong to more than one word class. **אמת**, for instance, is both a noun (**דער אמת**) and an adjective (**אמת'ע, אמת'ער**). Names of days, holidays, and seasons like **שבת** and **ווינטער** are both nouns (**דער שבת איז טייער**) and adverbs (**ער קומט שבת**).

<Note>
Adding a suffix to change a word's class creates a **new word**, not a dual-class word. **אמאל** (adverb) and **אמאליג** (adjective formed by adding **־יג**) are grammatically separate words that share a root. The word-class tests above apply to a word as it stands, without adding any extra suffixes or prefixes.
</Note>

### Infinitives used as nouns

The **אינפיניטיוו פארעם** (infinitive) of a verb is its name — e.g., **זאגן, רעדן, קומען**. Infinitives are frequently used as nouns and take the neuter article **דאס**: דאס זאגן, דאס רעדן, דאס שטיין.

### Gerundive forms as adjectives

Adding the suffix **־נדיג** or **־ענדיג** to a verb root creates a gerundive that functions as an adjective: זאגנדיגע, שטייענדיגע. These forms retain some verbal properties and are not classed as pure adjectives.

### Past-tense forms as adjectives

The past-tense form of nearly any verb can also serve as an adjective: **געזאגטע** ווערטער, **גערעדטע** זאכן, **געקומענע** געסט.

## איינסן — multi-word units

In grammar, sentences are divided not only into individual words but into **איינסן** (units). A unit is any word or group of words that carries a single meaning. Multi-word expressions that function as one unit are listed as single entries in dictionaries. Examples by class:

| Class | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **זאכווערטער** | ארץ־ישראל, יום־טוב, עלטער־זיידע, בייז־וואונדער |
| **ווערבן** | ליב האבן, פיינט האבן, חתונה האבן, זיך מרחם זיין |
| **קאניונקציעס** | נאכדעם וואס, צוליב דעם וואס, על כל פנים, ווי נאָר |
| **אדווערבן** | פרייטאג־צונאכטס, היינט־צוטאגס, פון דער נאנט |
