This page collects brief but important rules that are easy to overlook or get wrong. They cover spelling variants, word choice, verb forms, and grammar conventions. Use it as a quick-reference checklist when writing or proofreading Yiddish text.

## Spelling

### Suffixes: ־ליך vs. ־לעך

The suffixes ־ליך and ־לעך look similar but serve different grammatical roles and must not be confused.

| Suffix | Function | Example |
|--------|----------|---------|
| ־ליך | Adjective-forming suffix | מענטשליך (humane/human) |
| ־לעך | Plural suffix | טיכלעך (kerchiefs, pl. of טיכל) |

**צוביסלעך** is an adverb meaning "a little at a time" — it takes ־לעך because the ע is part of the correct adverbial form, not because it is a plural.

### Redundant letters: ה׳ and ע׳

Do not add a **ה׳** or **ע׳** at the end of a word if the word does not require it. Inserting extra letters "just in case" is a common hypercorrection. Check a dictionary if you are unsure whether a final ה׳ or ע׳ belongs.

### ־ן vs. ־ען endings

The infinitive form of a verb ends in either **־ן** or **־ען**. The same choice applies to certain noun and verb inflections. There are specific rules governing which ending is correct for a given word stem. When in doubt, consult a dictionary for the listed infinitive form.

### ליינען vs. לייענען

Write **ליינען** (to read), not לייענען. The form with the extra ע׳ is incorrect.

### געבויט vs. געבויעט

The past tense of בויען (to build) is **געבויט**, not געבויעט. Do not insert an extra ע׳ before the ט.

### באארבעט, not בעארבעט or ארבייט

The past tense of **באארבעטן** (to work on / process) is **באארבעט**. Two common errors:
- בעארבעט — incorrect spelling of the prefix (it is **בא־**, not **בע־**)
- ארבייט — this is the present-tense root of ארבעטן and is a different word entirely

## Word choice

### אלס vs. אלץ — and נאכאלץ

**אלס** and **אלץ** are distinct words with different meanings; they are not interchangeable. Likewise, **נאכאלץ** is its own word. Make sure you are using the form that matches the meaning you intend.

### פאר vs. איידער

Both words can translate as "before," but they are used differently:

| Word | Meaning / usage |
|------|-----------------|
| פאר | Before (temporal or spatial preposition) |
| איידער | Before / rather than — used in conjunctions and comparisons |

Use **פאר** as a preposition (e.g., פאר דעם — before that). Use **איידער** when introducing a clause or expressing "rather than."

### בעפאָר

**בעפאָר** is a separate word. Be aware of its specific usage and do not substitute it freely for פאר or איידער.

### זינט vs. זייט

These two words are sometimes confused:

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| זינט | Since (temporal — from a point in time until now) |
| זייט | Since / because (can also be used as a conjunction); also the plural/formal imperative of זיין |

Choose the correct word for the meaning you intend.

### Use דערפאר, not איבערדעם

When you want to say "therefore" or "for that reason," write **דערפאר** (or דעריבער). Avoid איבערדעם and similar forms for this purpose.

### כאטש alone suffices

You do not need to write **כאטש וואס**. The conjunction **כאטש** on its own already conveys the meaning "although / even though." Writing כאטש וואס is redundant.

### טראץ, טראצדעם, and better alternatives

| Form | Part of speech | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| טראץ | Preposition | "Despite" + noun |
| טראצדעם | Adverb | "Nevertheless / despite that" |
| טראצדעם וואס | Conjunction | "Although / despite the fact that" |

In many cases, shorter alternatives read more naturally:
- Instead of **טראצדעם** (adverb), consider **פונדעסטוועגן** — it is often more idiomatic.
- Instead of **טראצדעם וואס** (conjunction), consider **כאטש** — it is a single word and flows more easily.

## Conjunctions and compound conjunctions

### נאך וואס → נאכדעם וואס

Do not write נאך וואס as a conjunction meaning "after." The correct form is **נאכדעם וואס**.

Similarly, when constructing compound conjunctions with a preposition + דעם + וואס, you must include דעם:

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| נאך וואס | נאכדעם וואס |
| צוליב וואס | צוליב דעם וואס |

The same principle extends to other prepositional conjunctions of this type.

## Verb forms and syntax

### Word order with צו + infinitive

When **צו** is used before an infinitive (as a purposive or complement marker), it always comes **last** — directly before the main verb at the end of the clause.

If the verb has a **converb** (קאנווערב), צו comes **after the converb**, not before it. The converb and the main verb stay together as a unit.

### Do not separate converbs from their verbs

A converb and its verb are written as **one word**. Never split them across two words — doing so interrupts the flow of the text and is incorrect.

This rule is especially important in the past tense when **גע־** is added. The גע־ prefix goes **between** the converb and the verb root, and the whole thing remains one word:

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| אריינגעקומען | אריין געקומען |
| ארויסגעגאנגען | ארויס געגאנגען |
| אראפגעברענגט | אראפ געברענגט |

The converb with צו is also always one word: צוקומען, צוגיין, and so on.

### צו vs. צע prefixes

**צו־** and **צע־** are two different prefixes with different meanings. They are not interchangeable. Check that you are using the right one for the verb you are writing.

## Impersonal constructions

### עס + verb

Yiddish uses impersonal constructions with **עס** as a grammatical subject for natural events and similar expressions. The classic example is:

> **עס רעגנט** — It is raining.

The verb agrees with the grammatical subject עס (third person singular), even though there is no real-world subject performing the action. Apply this same pattern consistently with other impersonal verbs.
